When the GDPR regulations went into full force in May 2018, it came a bit like a shock. While people tried to make everything as GDPR-proof as possible, it felt as if no one really knew what is enough and how hard how hard possible fines would hit. I really enjoyed this week's [TidyTuesday](https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday) to explore the data to answer the question. 

I generated an annual map for the countries in the data and colored it by the cumulated fine (standardized by the cases per country). 

The code is available on my [GitHub](https://github.com/cosimameyer/TidyTuesday). You also find a larger version of the figure [here](https://github.com/cosimameyer/TidyTuesday/blob/master/figures/TidyTuesday_GDPR.png).
