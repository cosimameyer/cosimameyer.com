R is a great resource for data management, statistics, analysis, and visualization — and it becomes better every day. This is to a large part because of the active community that continuously creates and builds extensions for the R world. If you want to contribute to this community, writing a package can be one way. That is exactly what [Dennis Hammerschmidt](https://dennis-hammerschmidt.netlify.com/) and I showcase in our [newest blog post](https://www.mzes.uni-mannheim.de/socialsciencedatalab/article/r-package/) with our package [overviewR](https://cosimameyer.github.io/overviewR/). We provide a step-by-step guide — seasoned with new and helpful packages that are also inspired by presentations at the recent virtual [European R Users Meeting e-Rum 2020](https://2020.erum.io/).

The blog post is also featured as [a highlight in R Weekly](https://rweekly.org/2020-29.html).
